Our Approach

From AI uncertainty to confident action.

AI adoption isn't a tooling problem. It's a workforce transformation problem. Our three-phase approach helps your organisation move through it deliberately rather than reactively.

Phase 01

Diagnose

Through a workshop, leadership session, or fixed-scope readiness assessment, we identify where AI can create real value in your context and the workflows, capabilities and risks that need attention first.

Phase 02

Apply

We work alongside your people to put the highest-leverage use cases into practice using existing tools or helping you choose the right tools, designing real workflows, and proving value before you scale.

Phase 03

Embed

We help build the AI literacy, setup governance structures, and implement operating rhythms that make change stick so your teams keep moving long after the engagement ends.

About

Practitioner-led, not slide-deck-led.

Jami Emerson Cheneval is the principal of Cheneval Advisory, a Sydney-based consultancy helping Australian organisations adopt AI in practical, sustainable ways.

Originally from Oregon City in the United States, Jami completed a Bachelor of Information Technology at the Australian Catholic University and was named the 2020 NSW International Student of the Year — Higher Education for her leadership and contribution during her studies. She currently works in IT at the University of Technology Sydney as an AI Capability and Adoption Specialist and Test Automation Engineer.

Alongside her IT work, Jami is the founder of an Australian wholefood snack bar brand built on lean operations and integrated AI workflows, and she served as the 2020 Founder-in-Residence at the UTS Techcelerator Female Founder Mentoring Program, supporting emerging women founders across the Australian innovation community.

Through Cheneval Advisory, Jami brings a practitioner's perspective to AI transformation — combining technical engineering discipline, founder experience, and hands-on institutional AI adoption work. She believes AI adoption is a workforce transformation challenge, not a tooling one, and works particularly closely with leaders in education and people-and-culture functions to make it real.
